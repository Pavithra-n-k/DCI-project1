ISKENDER KEBAB

KEBAB WITH YOGHURT

Iskender From Turkey

Iskender kebab originated in Bursa, Turkey, in the 19th century. It was invented by a chef named Iskender Efendi. Inspired by döner kebab, Iskender Efendi created a unique variation using thinly sliced lamb or beef cooked on a rotisserie. The meat was served on pita bread, topped with tomato sauce and melted butter, and accompanied by yogurt and grilled green peppers. Iskender kebab gained popularity for its succulent meat and flavorful combination of ingredients. Today, it is enjoyed in various parts of Turkey and around the world, representing the rich culinary heritage of Turkey and the innovation of Iskender Efendi.

INGREDIENTS

- Lamb or beef: Thinly sliced, tender, and juicy meat.

- Pita bread: Freshly baked pita bread.

- Tomato sauce: A rich and flavorful tomato-based sauce.

- Butter: Melted butter.

- Yogurt: Creamy yogurt.

- Grilled green peppers: Mildly spicy grilled green peppers.

PREPARATION

  1. Start by preparing the tomato sauce. In a saucepan, combine tomatoes, garlic, onions, and your choice of herbs and spices. Simmer over medium heat until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld together.

  2. While the sauce is simmering, grill or cook the thinly sliced lamb or beef until it is cooked through and tender. Set the cooked meat aside.

  3. Warm the pita bread either on a grill or in the oven, ensuring it is soft and pliable.

  4. To assemble the Iskender Kebab, place a layer of warm pita bread on a serving plate.

  5. Arrange the cooked meat slices over the pita bread, creating an even layer.

  6. Generously drizzle the tomato sauce over the meat and pita bread, allowing the flavors to soak in.

  7. Finish by pouring melted butter over the top, ensuring it coats the meat and sauce.

  8. Serve the Iskender Kebab with a side of creamy yogurt and grilled green peppers.
